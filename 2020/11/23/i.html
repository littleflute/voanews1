The first Americans could receive a COVID-19 vaccine as soon as December 11, the chief scientific adviser for the U.S. government's vaccine program said on Sunday.

Moncef Slaoui told NBC's "Meet the Press" news show that within 24 hours from approval, the vaccine will be moving to where it's needed most.

He said about 70 percent of the population needs to be immunized to achieve herd immunity, a goal the country could reach by May.

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration's outside advisers will meet on December 10 to discuss whether to authorize the COVID-19 vaccine developed by Pfizer Incorporated and German partner BioNTech for emergency use.



Spain will begin a comprehensive coronavirus vaccination program in January, Prime Minister Pedro Sanchez said on Sunday. He said Spain and Germany were the first European Union countries to have a complete vaccination plan in place.



Australia's top military official said on Sunday the country's defense force must "own" a recent report on soldiers committing crimes in Afghanistan and pledged changes to ensure that atrocities do not happen again.

The report was published on Thursday after an inquiry into the conduct of special forces personnel in Afghanistan between 2005 and 2016. It found that senior commandos forced junior soldiers to kill [defense...] defenseless captives in order to prepare them for combat in an act called "blooding."



A newly elected MP for Myanmar's ruling [par...] party has been shot dead by an unidentified gunman in northern Shan state and the party on Sunday urged a quick investigation. It also denounced political violence in the aftermath of elections this month.



Thousands of people took to Taipei's streets on Sunday for the annual "Autumn Struggle" protest march organized by labor groups, with much of the anger focused on the government's decision to ease restrictions on imports of U.S. pork.

Taiwan's main opposition party, the Kuomintang, or KMT, rallied its supporters to join the march for the first time, having mounted an increasingly strident campaign against the pork decision, which it says threatens food safety.



Afghanistan faces funding cuts and tighter restrictions on vital aid from an international donor conference this week hosted in Geneva, marking further challenges for a nation torn by two decades of war and now ravaged by COVID-19.

Ministers from about 70 countries and officials of humanitarian organizations will meet virtually early next week. They are expected to pledge billions of dollars to safeguard development projects, with talks between the Afghan government and Taliban rebels stalled and President Trump reducing forces.
