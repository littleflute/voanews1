A NASA rover intended to search for life on Mars successfully landed on Thursday. We get more from AP's Tim McGuire.

NASA ground controller Swati Mohan delivered the news to the Perseverance team at NASA's Jet Propulsion Labs in Pasadena, California.

"Touchdown confirmed! Perseverance is safely on the surface of Mars."

Later after the six-wheeled rover had been on the surface for a while, Mohan delivered a key announcement.

"Perseverance is alive on the surface of Mars. Congratulations to the mission ... the first image."

Over the next two years, Perseverance will use its 7-foot arm to drill down and collect rock samples with possible signs of bygone microscopic life. Three to four dozen samples will be sealed in tubes and set aside to be retrieved by a fetch rover and brought to Earth by another rocket ship as early as 2031.

I'm Tim McGuire.

A new U.N. report says humans are making Earth a broken and increasingly unlivable planet through climate change, biodiversity loss and pollution.

U.N. Secretary-General António Guterres said 2021 is what he called a make-or-break year.

"Human well-being lies in protecting the health of the planet. And it's time to re-evaluate and reset our relationship with nature."

Unlike past U.N. reports that focused on one issue and avoided telling leader actions to take, Thursday's report combines three intertwined environmental crises and tells the world what's got to change.

It calls for changing what governments tax, how nations value economic input, how power is generated, the way people get around, the way they fish and farm, as well as what they eat.



NATO Secretary-General Jens Stoltenberg said Thursday defense ministers had made no final decision on its military presence in Afghanistan.

Speaking at a virtual news briefing in Brussels, Stoltenberg said that the mission cannot resolve itself until peace talks are made.

"The peace process is the best chance to end years of suffering and violence, and bring lasting peace."

NATO has just under 10 thousand troops in the war-ravaged country helping to train and to advise the Afghan security forces.



Daily protests continued across Myanmar on Thursday. We get more from Reuters Libby Hogan.

The Myanmar military issued arrest warrants for six celebrities who are accused of encouraging civil servants to strike and join the protests against this month's coup.

The strikes have paralyzed many government offices and businesses.

But one name on the warrant list, actor Lu Min, posted defiantly on his Facebook page that, quote, "It's amazing to see the unity of our people. People power must return to the people."

Despite junta appeals for civil servants to return to work and threats if they do not, there has been no sign of the strikes easing.

Motorists in Yangon also continued with a campaign to show their opposition to the coup by driving at a snail pace through downtown traffic on Thursday.

Nearly 500 people have been arrested for taking part in the protests.

That's Reuters Libby Hogan.
