American employers scaled back hiring last month as the pandemic got worse. AP's Sagar Meghani has more.

For fifth straight month, hiring was down with employers adding just 245,000 jobs in November, the fewest since April.

"It does appear that businesses perhaps have turned more cautious. We have seen rising caseloads, record caseloads of the coronavirus. We are starting to see some business closures and layoffs again."

Gus Faucher at PNC Financial Services Group says the unemployment rate fell to 6.7 percent - good news in and of itself but most are reflection that many people simply stopped looking for work and no longer counted as unemployed.

Faucher predicts even slower job growth and maybe losses before a coronavirus vaccine starts to help stabilize the economy.

Sagar Meghani, Washington.

The World Health Organization says the world is close to seeing the end of the COVID-19 pandemic, but that inequalities in distributing a vaccine and other therapeutics could have deep economic and social consequences.

Dr. Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus, the WHO's director-general, told the U.N. General Assembly Friday that "the light at the end of the tunnel is growing steadily brighter."

Ghebreyesus said in order to prevent further inequalities, all vaccines and therapeutics "must be shared equitably as global public goods," and "not as private commodities."

"This is a global crisis and the solutions must be shared equitably as global public goods."

The WHO director-general stressed that funding for the ACT Accelerator, the global collaboration to develop COVID-19 diagnostics, therapeutics and vaccines faces a funding gap of more than $4 billion for the current year and an additional 23.9 billion in 2021.



The Pentagon said Friday it is pulling most U.S. troops out of Somalia on President Donald Trump's orders continuing a post election push by Trump to shrink U.S. involvement in counterterrorism missions abroad.

There are currently 700 troops in the Horn of African nation, training and advising local forces in an extended fight against the extremist group, al-Shabab, an affiliate of al-Qaeda.



Talks between the United Kingdom and the European Union have come to a halt as Britain approaches the end of its transition out of the EU. AP's Ben Thomas reports.

The European Union's chief negotiator says he and his British counterpart have agreed to "pause" negotiations in order to brief their political leaders.

Michel Barnier says the conditions for a trade deal have not been met and differences remain after a week of intense negotiations.

Barnier says British Prime Minister Boris Johnson and EU Commission President Ursula von der Leyen will discuss the state of the talks Saturday.

The United Kingdom left the EU early this year but remains part of the bloc's economic embrace during an 11-month transition as the two sides try to negotiate a new free-trade deal that will take effect January 1.

I'm Ben Thomas.



U.S. Secretary of State Mike Pompeo on Friday said Iran was "desperately" signaling its willingness to return to the negotiation table to get sanctions relief. President Donald Trump withdrew the United States from the previous Iran nuclear deal.

President-elect Joe Biden, who is set to office on January 20, has said he will return the United States to the Obama-era deal if Iran resumes compliance with the agreement.
